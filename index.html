Lock-in

Be on the lookout for lock-in. Many of the vendors out there have great Saas, but who owns your data and where it it stored?

Follow the Open Core principle as much as you can. Pay for support, maintenance but not for licenses. It's what you believe in with Drupal, Apache Solr, Mautic, Nginx, etc... Why settle for less when it comes to data?

Capture + Rules + Elastic Search = Quick segmenting, real time. Make sure you take the Open DXP Approach. Do not allow Saas to hijack your Open Journey. I suggest to read up on the Open Core model. We take it for Granted that Drupal is open. Why not demand that the software where we put all our visitor data is also yours. And don't get me wrong, owning your software doesn't mean you should self-host. Make sure companies are liable for the security and the integrity unless you have the power to take this challenge on. Why would we build our entire ecosystem on Drupal to then allow corporations to capture the data and embedding ourselves in a vendor lock-in. Mind you, this is not only the case with Acquia but with all the vendors on the Magic Quadrant.
